What is a CV (Curriculum Vitae)?

CV (short for Curriculum Vitae) is the Latin word for "course of life."

In a nutshell, a CV is a summary of who you are as a professional, including your work history, education, and skills. Done right, a CV is supposed to “sell you” to potential employers and land you a job.

What’s the Difference Between a CV and a Resume?

Technically speaking, a CV and a resume are the same document. The only difference is that Americans call it a "resume," while Europeans and others refer to it as a CV.

A European CV can be between two and three pages long, depending on experience. A US resume is usually one or two pages max.

For more on the CV vs Resume topic, check out our article.

How to Write a CV?

There’s a lot that goes into writing an attention-grabbing CV, so you’re better off reading our comprehensive guide to improve your chances of getting hired.

    That said, here are some effective tips to get you started:
  • Pick a CV template that suits your industry.
  • Keep the layout professional with consistent headings, white space, and a readable font.
  • Add essential contact details (name, job title, phone number, email, LinkedIn).
  • Write a concise summary highlighting your top achievements.
  • Showcase work experience with quantifiable achievements.
  • Keep the education section brief, mentioning only the highest degree.
  • List relevant soft and hard skills.
  • Optionally, include sections like certifications, languages, or personal projects.
  • Follow the tips in our CV builder to help you polish your CV.

How Long Should a CV Be?

For most jobs, a CV can be between one and three pages. That said, we always recommend keeping your CV short and to the point (i.e. one page long). The more concise you make your CV, the more likely it is for the hiring manager to read it from start to finish. If you’re based in the US, though, and you’re applying for a job or admission in academia, your CV can be as long as it needs to reflect your lengthy academic career (i.e. eight pages or more).
